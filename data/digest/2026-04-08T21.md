## 中文摘要

### 核心事件

伊朗总统佩泽什基安将停火协议称为已故最高领袖哈梅内伊的"血之果实"，显示伊朗强硬立场；尽管宣布停火，霍尔木兹海峡通行问题依然存在，伊朗海军仍要求船只获得许可才能通行。

### 政治表态

- **伊朗总统佩泽什基安**：在社交媒体上将停火协议称为"最高领袖阿里·哈梅内伊的血之果实"，强调从今天起将继续在"外交、国防和服务"方面团结一致
- **美国防长海格赛**：声称伊朗"乞求"停火，美军曾处于"锁定并装弹"状态准备打击伊朗关键基础设施
- **欧盟外交代表卡拉斯**：将美伊协议描述为"从悬崖边缘后退一步"，为持久和平创造空间

### 军事动态

- **霍尔木兹海峡**：航运缓慢恢复，已有两艘货轮通过，但仍有数百艘船只滞留
- **伊朗岛屿爆炸**：拉万岛炼油厂附近发生多次爆炸，原因不明；锡里岛也报告爆炸
- **阿联酋响应**：防空系统击退来袭导弹群，阿布扎比天然气加工设施起火
- **约旦拦截**：过去24小时内拦截两枚伊朗导弹

### 经济反应

- **油价大跌**：美油下跌近20%，布伦特原油下跌16%，均跌至每桶95美元左右
- **股市大涨**：首尔股市涨6.9%，东京涨5.4%，全球股市普遍上涨
- **海湾股市**：迪拜股市上涨8.5%，为2014年12月以来最大单日涨幅

### 外交进展

- **日本和韩国**：宣布确保船只安全通过霍尔木兹海峡的努力
- **埃及**：称停火是"将战争完全结束"的重要步骤
- **联合国海事组织**：优先确保船只安全通过霍尔木兹海峡

### 分析判断

尽管停火协议已达成，但双方言辞显示信任基础薄弱。伊朗将协议描述为"胜利"，而美国则强调军事压力迫使伊朗让步。霍尔木兹海峡的实际开放面临挑战，伊朗仍维持"许可"要求，与特朗普的"立即完全开放"要求存在差距。短期内协议可能维持，但长期和平仍需解决深层次矛盾。

## English Summary

### Core Events

Iranian President Pezeshkian described the ceasefire agreement as the "fruit of the blood" of late Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei, signaling a hardline Iranian stance; despite the ceasefire announcement, transit issues persist in the Strait of Hormuz as Iran's navy still requires ships to obtain permission for passage.

### Political Statements

- **Iranian President Pezeshkian**: On social media, called the ceasefire "the fruit of the blood" of Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei, emphasizing that from today they will continue to "stand together" in diplomacy, defense, and service
- **US Defense Secretary Hegseth**: Claimed Iran "begged for this ceasefire" and that the US military was "locked and loaded" to strike key Iranian infrastructure before the agreement
- **EU Foreign Representative Kallas**: Described the US-Iran agreement as "a step back from the brink," creating space for lasting peace

### Military Developments

- **Strait of Hormuz**: Shipping slowly resuming with two vessels transiting, but hundreds of ships remain stranded
- **Iranian Island Explosions**: Multiple explosions heard near Lavan Island oil refinery, cause unclear; Siri Island also reported explosions
- **UAE Response**: Air defense systems intercepted incoming missile barrages, Abu Dhabi gas processing facility caught fire
- **Jordan Interceptions**: Two Iranian missiles intercepted over the past 24 hours

### Economic Reactions

- **Oil Price Drop**: US crude down nearly 20%, Brent down 16%, both trading around $95 per barrel
- **Stock Market Surge**: Seoul markets up 6.9%, Tokyo up 5.4%, global markets broadly higher
- **Gulf Markets**: Dubai stocks jumped 8.5%, biggest intraday increase since December 2014

### Diplomatic Developments

- **Japan and South Korea**: Announced efforts to ensure their ships can safely pass through the Strait of Hormuz
- **Egypt**: Called the ceasefire "a significant step towards" bringing the war to a "complete end"
- **UN Maritime Organization**: Priority is ensuring ships can leave the Strait of Hormuz safely

### Analysis

Despite the ceasefire agreement being reached, statements from both sides indicate a weak foundation of trust. Iran describes the agreement as a "victory," while the US emphasizes military pressure forced Iran's concession. The actual opening of the Strait of Hormuz faces challenges as Iran maintains its "permission" requirement, conflicting with Trump's demand for "COMPLETE, IMMEDIATE, and SAFE OPENING." The agreement may hold in the short term, but lasting peace requires resolving deeper contradictions.
