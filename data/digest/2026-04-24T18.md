- 宣布将黎以停火从4月16日延长三周
- 特朗普表示希望与伊朗达成"最佳协议"，"不想仓促行事"

## English Summary

### Core Events

Following a second round of peace talks in Washington, President Trump announced that the Israel-Lebanon ceasefire agreement has been extended by three weeks, marking significant progress in US-Iran peace negotiations.

### Military Developments

- Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps posted dramatic video of what it claimed were commandos boarding ships in the Strait of Hormuz
- Philippine government confirmed 15 seafarers are being held by Iran's Revolutionary Guard but described them as "safe and unharmed"
- US is sending thousands more troops to the region
- Lebanese journalist Amal Khalil was killed in Israeli airstrike on southern village of al-Tiri

### Diplomatic Developments

- Trump held second round of peace talks with Lebanon in Washington
- Announced extension of Israel-Lebanon ceasefire by three weeks (from April 16)
- Trump stated he wants to make the "best deal possible" with Iran, later adding "I don't want to rush myself"
- Pakistan pushing for new round of direct US-Iran peace talks

### Analysis

Positive signs emerging in US-Iran peace talks with the ceasefire extension providing a valuable diplomatic window. Trump's "I don't want to rush myself" comment suggests a preference for diplomatic resolution.
