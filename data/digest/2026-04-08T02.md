### 核心事件

- 特朗普称伊朗是"积极、愿意参与"谈判的参与者，但拒绝回答战争是否接近结束
- 伊朗提交10条款回应，要求结束地区敌对、建立霍尔木兹海峡安全通道、重建、解除制裁
- 伊朗安全官员称已给予特朗普"战略失败"，其升级言论反映了冲突失控
- 阿曼就霍尔木兹海峡问题与伊朗进行会谈，巴基斯坦和埃及保持华盛顿与德黑兰沟通渠道

## English Summary

### Core Events

President Trump issued extreme threats, declaring Iran could be "taken out in one night," and set an April 7 deadline at 8 PM for Iran to fully reopen the Strait of Hormuz. Tehran firmly rejected a temporary ceasefire proposal, demanding permanent end to the war and compensation for war damages, putting US-Iran talks in deadlock.

### Military Developments

- Israel struck South Pars petrochemical complex in southern Iran, rendering 85% of Iran's petrochemical export facilities inoperative
- Trump threatened to destroy all Iranian energy facilities and bridges within 24 hours
- US rescue operation resulted in death of 4 Iranian officers
- Iran threatened to strike AI center in UAE in retaliation
- Lebanon conflict has killed 1,497 people, Israel ordering southern residents to evacuate immediately
- Israel approved updated target list of energy and infrastructure sites in Iran

### Diplomatic Developments

- Trump called Iran an "active, willing participant" in negotiations but refused to say whether war is winding down
- Iran submitted 10-clause response demanding end to regional hostilities, safe passage through Strait of Hormuz, reconstruction, and sanctions lifting
- Iranian security officials claimed to have dealt Trump a "strategic defeat," with escalating rhetoric reflecting loss of control over conflict
- Oman held talks with Iran about Strait of Hormuz, while Pakistan and Egypt maintained communication channels between Washington and Tehran
- Trump claimed US is the "winner" and should impose tolls in strait, not Iran

### Analysis

The hardline positions of both US and Iran on the Strait of Hormuz issue have led to a negotiation deadlock. Trump's extreme rhetoric threatens further escalation, while Iran's firm response indicates unwillingness to yield easily. Regional conflict is expanding with deteriorating Lebanon situation and worsening humanitarian crisis. Rising oil prices reflect market concerns over escalating tensions.
