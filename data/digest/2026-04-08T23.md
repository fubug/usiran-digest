## 中文摘要

### 核心事件

美伊两国初步达成两周停火协议并开始实施，但以色列继续在黎巴嫩对真主党发动大规模空袭，停火局面依然脆弱。

### 军事动态

- 美伊停火协议初步生效，航运活动有所恢复（已有2艘散货船通过霍尔木兹海峡）
- 以色列在贝鲁特及周边地区对真主党发动战争爆发以来最大规模的空袭
- 科威特国防部称，尽管有停火，但拦截了来自伊朗的至少28架无人机
- 伊朗媒体称拉兰岛炼油厂遭"不明敌人"袭击，伊朗在波斯湾国家再次发动攻击
- 美国防长赫格塞要求伊朗交出970磅高浓缩铀储备

### 外交进展

- 巴基斯坦总理谢巴兹·谢里夫证实邀请美伊代表团周五在伊斯兰堡举行会谈
- 伊朗国家安全委员会表示将参加在伊斯兰堡的谈判
- 美国表示正在考虑与伊朗进行面对面谈判
- 以色列内塔尼亚胡被批评者称为"外交灾难"，指责其未能实现摧毁伊朗神权政府的目标

### 分析判断

停火协议表面生效但存在多重脆弱性：以色列明确拒绝在黎巴嫩方向停火，伊朗仍在波斯湾地区发动攻击，且双方在核设施问题上的立场依然对立。短期内仍可能发生小规模冲突，但全面战争升级风险暂时降低。原油市场对停火反应积极，预计油价将进一步回落。

## English Summary

### Core Events

A fragile ceasefire between the US and Iran appears to be holding as of April 8, but Israel continues large-scale strikes against Hezbollah in Lebanon, indicating the ceasefire remains fragile.

### Military Developments

- US-Iran ceasefire has begun implementation with some shipping activity resuming (at least 2 bulk carriers crossed the Strait of Hormuz)
- Israel conducted its largest strike against Hezbollah since that front opened up following the militant group's rocket attacks on Israel in March
- Kuwait's defense ministry said it engaged with at least 28 drones from Iran despite the ceasefire
- Iranian media reported that an oil refinery on Lavan island was struck by "unspecified enemies"
- US Defense Secretary Hegseth called on Iran to turn over its 970 pounds of highly enriched uranium

### Diplomatic Developments

- Pakistan's Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif confirmed he has invited US and Iranian delegations for talks in Islamabad on Friday
- Iran's National Security Council said it would attend the Islamabad talks
- The Trump administration said it was in discussions about holding in-person talks with Iran
- Critics of Netanyahu called the ceasefire "a diplomatic disaster," accusing him of failing to achieve his stated war goal

### Analysis

The ceasefire is superficially holding but has multiple vulnerabilities: Israel explicitly refuses to cease operations in Lebanon, Iran continues attacks in Persian Gulf nations, and both sides remain entrenched on nuclear issues. Small-scale conflicts are likely in the short term, but the risk of full-scale war escalation has temporarily diminished. Oil markets responded positively to the ceasefire, with prices expected to continue falling.
