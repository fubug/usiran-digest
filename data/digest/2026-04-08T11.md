## 中文摘要

### 核心事件

美国和伊朗达成为期两周的停火协议，以色列总理内塔尼亚胡表示该协议不包含在黎巴嫩对真主党的战斗。这一协议由巴基斯坦斡旋达成，在特朗普原定最后期限前紧急达成。

### 军事动态

- 以色列军方官员表示该国仍在攻击伊朗
- 以色列总理内塔尼亚胡支持美国与伊朗的停火协议，但表示不包括对黎巴嫩真主党的战斗
- 美国暂停对伊朗的军事行动
- 伊朗表示已接受两周停火，将在巴基斯坦伊斯兰堡开始谈判

### 外交进展

- 巴基斯坦成功斡旋达成停火协议
- 特朗普总统在最后期限前暂停对伊朗的攻击威胁
- 伊朗和联合国就停火协议进行谈判
- 澳大利亚谴责特朗普威胁摧毁伊朗文明的言论不合适
- 多个亚洲和太平洋国家政府对停火表示欢迎

## English Summary

### Core Events

The United States and Iran have reached a two-week ceasefire agreement, with Israeli Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu stating that the deal does not include fighting against Hezbollah in Lebanon. This Pakistan-brokered deal was reached urgently before President Trump's original deadline.

### Military Developments

- Israeli military officials say the country is still attacking Iran
- Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu supports US ceasefire with Iran but states it doesn't cover fighting against Hezbollah
- United States suspends military attacks on Iran
- Iran says it has accepted the two-week ceasefire with negotiations to begin in Pakistan

### Diplomatic Developments

- Pakistan successfully brokered the ceasefire agreement
- President Trump suspended attack threats against Iran before the deadline
- Iran and UN negotiate ceasefire terms
- Australia condemns Trump's threats to destroy Iranian civilization as inappropriate
- Multiple Asian and Pacific Pacific governments welcome the ceasefire

### Analysis

The ceasefire agreement marks a temporary easing of US-Iran tensions, but Israel's non-cooperative attitude suggests the root causes of the conflict remain unresolved. This ceasefire is more of a tactical pause than a long-term solution, especially considering Israel's explicit commitment to continue military action against Hezbollah.
