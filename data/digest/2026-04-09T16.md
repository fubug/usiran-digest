## English Summary

### Core Events

President Trump ordered reinforcement of US military forces in the Middle East and threatened "bigger, better, and stronger" military action, while large-scale Israeli airstrikes in Lebanon killed at least 254 people, with the UN condemning the attacks as "horrific," putting the ceasefire agreement under severe pressure.

### Military Developments

- Trump announced all US military ships, aircraft and personnel will remain in the Middle East, warning if the agreement is not fully complied with, fighting will resume "bigger, and better, and stronger than anyone has seen before"
- Lebanese health ministry confirmed Israeli airstrikes on Wednesday killed at least 254 people and injured 1,165, the highest single-day toll in the Israel-Hezbollah war
- Iranian Foreign Minister Abdollahian accused Israel of violating the ceasefire, demanding the US choose between "ceasefire or continued war via Israel"
- No oil tankers have crossed the Strait of Hormuz since the ceasefire, with shipping companies citing "instability" for not committing to transit

### Diplomatic Developments

- UN High Commissioner for Human Rights Volker Turk described Israeli attacks on Lebanon as "horrific," occurring soon after the US-Iran ceasefire announcement
- Pakistan will host peace talks in Islamabad on Saturday, with the Iranian delegation expected Thursday night
- White House clarified ceasefire doesn't include Lebanon, calling it a "reasonable misunderstanding"
- UAE demanded Iran pay for damages to Gulf neighbors, seeking assurances Tehran will cease hostilities

### Analysis

The US-Iran ceasefire faces immediate and serious challenges, with Trump's military buildup threat and increased civilian casualties in Lebanon suggesting the agreement may not hold. Regional tensions are unlikely to ease in the short term, with the blocked Strait of Hormuz continuing to impact global energy markets. Saturday's peace talks in Pakistan will be crucial to the agreement's survival.
