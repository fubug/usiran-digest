## 中文摘要

### 核心事件

- **CENTCOM拦截船只数据更新**：美国中央司令部宣布，针对伊朗的封锁行动中已累计拦截31艘船只，较前一日增加3艘。这一数字更新发生在4月22日21:07（北京时间4月23日5:07）。
- **伊朗扣押霍尔木兹海峡两艘货船**：伊朗伊斯兰革命卫队(IRGC)对三艘货船发动攻击，扣押希腊籍"Epaminondas"和巴拿马籍"MSC-Francesca"，指控其"未经授权通行且篡改导航系统"。英国海事机构确认其中一艘船遭"严重损坏"。

### 军事动态

- 美国海军持续封锁伊朗港口，CENTCOM拦截船只数从28艘增至31艘
- 伊斯兰革命卫队发布突击队登船扣押货船的戏剧性画面
- 美国海军部长John Phelan突然离职，由海军次长Hung Cao代理
- 特朗普政府内部人事变动频繁，数周前国防部长Hegseth也曾要求陆军参谋长辞职

### 外交进展

- 伊朗总统Pezeshkian在社交媒体表示："违反承诺、封锁和威胁是真正谈判的主要障碍"
- 白宫称伊朗扣押商船不违反停火协议，理由是"既不是美国船也不是以色列船"
- 美国参议院再次否决限制特朗普对伊战争权力的提案，46-51票未能通过
- 伊朗议会发言人Ghalibaf重申"不可能重新开放海峡"，指责美国封锁违反停火

### 分析判断

美伊冲突进入"数字战"阶段，双方通过数据发布和心理战进行博弈。CENTCOM突然更新拦截船只数量，可能是为了强化封锁效果的心理威慑。与此同时，伊朗通过扣押商船展示维持霍尔木兹控制能力。这种小规模的持续对抗虽避免了大规模军事冲突，但经济影响正在全球蔓延：美国海军人事动荡、伊朗经济受压、全球供应链紧张。这场消耗战的持久性表明，除非5月中美峰会取得突破，否则僵局可能持续数月。

## English Summary

### Core Events

- **CENTCOM interception count update**: U.S. Central Command announced the total number of vessels intercepted in the Iran blockade has risen to 31, an increase of 3 from the previous day. This update was issued at 21:07 on April 22 (Beijing time 05:07 on April 23).
- **Iran seizes two cargo ships in Strait of Hormuz**: Iran's Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC) attacked three cargo vessels and seized the Greek-owned Epaminondas and Panama-flagged MSC-Francesca, accusing them of "operating without authorization and tampering with navigation systems." A British maritime agency confirmed one vessel was "heavily damaged" by Iranian forces.

### Military Developments

- U.S. Navy continues blockade of Iranian ports, with CENTCOM interception count increasing from 28 to 31 ships
- IRGC released dramatic footage of commandos boarding and seizing the cargo ships
- Navy Secretary John Phelan abruptly resigned, with Navy Undersecretary Hung Cao serving as acting secretary
- Trump administration continues internal personnel changes, following Defense Secretary Hegseth's request for Army Chief Randy George to step down weeks earlier

### Diplomatic Developments

- Iranian President Pezeshkian stated on social media: "Breach of commitments, blockade and threats are main obstacles to genuine negotiations"
- White House claimed Iran's seizure of commercial ships doesn't violate the ceasefire, arguing "they are not U.S. ships, not Israeli ships"
- U.S. Senate again rejected an attempt to limit Trump's war powers against Iran, with the vote 46-51 against the measure
- Iranian Parliament Speaker Ghalibaf reiterated "reopening the strait is not possible" due to U.S. blockade violations

### Analysis

The US-Iran conflict has entered a "digital war" phase, with both sides engaging in psychological warfare through data releases and public messaging. The sudden CENTCOM update of the interception count appears aimed at enhancing the psychological impact of the blockade. Meanwhile, Iran demonstrates its ability to maintain control over the Hormuz Strait by seizing commercial ships. This low-level continuous confrontation, while avoiding large-scale military conflict, is creating global economic impacts: U.S. Navy leadership turmoil, Iranian economic pressure, and global supply chain tensions. The durability of this attritional war suggests that without a breakthrough at the upcoming May US-China summit, the stalemate could persist for months.
