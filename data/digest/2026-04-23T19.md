### Analysis

The US-Iran conflict has reached a delicate balance, with both sides conducting limited operations to demonstrate strength while avoiding full-scale war. The sudden increase in CENTCOM ship interceptions and the Iranian president's hardline statements suggest new obstacles to the negotiation process. Frequent leadership changes within the U.S. military reflect internal divisions over war strategy. European nations are beginning to feel tangible economic impacts from the conflict, with Germany's massive aviation adjustments signaling potential global supply chain restructuring.
