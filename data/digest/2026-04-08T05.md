## 中文摘要

### 核心事件

特朗普发出极端威胁称"整个文明今晚将死亡"，威胁若伊朗不重新开放霍尔木兹海峡，将摧毁伊朗所有桥梁和发电厂。伊朗宣布退出谈判，并通过无人机和导弹扩大反制，袭击海湾国家能源设施。

### 军事动态

- 特朗普威胁摧毁伊朗"整个文明"，称"今晚将永久死亡"
- 美军对哈尔克岛发动90多次"重新打击"，确保目标受损
- 以色列空袭伊朗境内8座桥梁，警告当地居民晚上9点前勿乘坐铁路
- 伊朗向沙特和阿联酋发射导弹和无人机，针对美盟友能源设施
- 伊朗民众在桥梁和发电厂周围组成"人链"进行示威

### 外交进展

- 伊朗宣布退出与美国的间接谈判，拒绝特朗普的最后通牒
- 巴基斯坦作为调解方呼吁停火，推动延期两周谈判
- 美国和以色列对谈判进展说法不一，官方称有进展
- 英国召集40多个国家军事规划会议，讨论战后霍尔木兹海峡安全
- 联合国秘书长和教皇均谴责威胁平民基础设施的行为

## English Summary

### Core Events

President Trump issued extreme threats including a "whole civilization will die tonight" ultimatum, warning Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz or face destruction of all bridges and power plants. Iran withdrew from negotiations and escalated countermeasures with drone and missile attacks against energy facilities in Gulf nations.

### Military Developments

- Trump threatened to destroy Iran's "whole civilization," stating it would "never be brought back again"
- US launched more than 90 "restrike" attacks on Kharg Island to ensure maximum damage
- Israel conducted airstrikes on 8 bridges across Iran, warning citizens not to use railways until 9 p.m. local time
- Iran fired missiles and drones at Saudi Arabia and UAE, targeting allied energy facilities
- Iranian civilians formed human chains around bridges and power plants in protest

### Diplomatic Developments

- Iran announced withdrawal from indirect talks with Trump administration, rejecting the ultimatum
- Pakistan as mediator called for ceasefire and pushed for two-week negotiation extension
- US and Israel gave conflicting statements on negotiation progress
- Britain convened meeting with 40+ countries' military planners for post-war Strait security
- UN Secretary-General and Pope condemned threats to civilian infrastructure

Trump's extreme rhetoric marks a dangerous escalation in the conflict, with Iran's withdrawal from negotiations signaling firm resistance. Large-scale counterattacks indicate continued deterioration with no clear signs of de-escalation. Growing international attention to civilian casualties complicates future mediation efforts.
