## 中文摘要

### 核心事件

伊朗外交部长公开指责美国和以色列应为霍尔木兹海峡当前的紧张局势负责，称美以行动违反了国际法。国际社会持续关注霍尔木兹海峡这一全球石油运输要道的局势发展。

### 军事动态

- 伊斯兰革命卫队在霍尔木兹海峡继续保持高度戒备状态
- 美国海军继续在相关海域巡逻，执行对伊朗船只的拦截任务
- 英国军方报告霍尔木兹海峡局势依然紧张，商船通行面临风险
- 五角大楼官员表示清除霍尔木兹海峡水雷的工作仍在继续

### 外交进展

- 伊朗外交部发言人强调美国自4月13日起对霍尔木兹海峡的海上 blockade 违反了停火协议
- 伊朗拒绝在伊斯兰堡举行第二轮和平谈判，指责美方从一开始就违反了停火
- 欧盟继续关注局势，但尚未提出新的调解方案
- 联合国呼吁各方保持克制，避免局势进一步升级

### 分析判断

伊朗外长的表态显示伊朗继续通过外交渠道向国际社会传递其立场，将霍尔木兹海峡的责任归咎于美国和以色列。这一策略旨在争取国际支持，同时展示伊朗对自身权益的坚持。霍尔木兹海峡作为全球石油运输的关键通道，其局势稳定不仅关系到中东安全，更影响全球经济。当前局势处于危险的平衡状态，双方都在展示力量但避免直接冲突，然而任何误判都可能打破这种平衡。

## English Summary

### Core Events

Iran's Foreign Minister publicly blamed the United States and Israel for the current tensions in the Strait of Hormuz, stating that their actions violate international law. The international community continues to monitor developments in this critical global oil shipping corridor.

### Military Developments

- Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps maintains high alert status in the Strait of Hormuz
- US Navy continues patrols in the area, carrying out interception operations against Iranian vessels
- British military reports Strait of Hormuz situation remains tense with risks to commercial shipping
- Pentagon officials indicate mine clearance operations in the Strait of Hormuz continue

### Diplomatic Developments

- Iranian Foreign Ministry spokesman emphasized that the US naval blockade of the Strait of Hormuz since April 13 violated the ceasefire agreement
- Iran refused to hold a second round of peace talks in Islamabad, accusing the US of violating the ceasefire from the beginning
- EU continues to monitor the situation but has not proposed new mediation initiatives
- United Nations urges all parties to exercise restraint and avoid further escalation

### Analysis

Iran's Foreign Minister's statement demonstrates Iran's ongoing strategy to communicate its position to the international community, attributing responsibility for the Strait of Hormuz situation to the US and Israel. This approach aims to gain international support while demonstrating Iran's commitment to its rights and interests. The Strait of Hormuz, as a critical global oil shipping channel, its stability affects not only Middle East security but the global economy. The current situation exists in a dangerous equilibrium where both sides display strength but avoid direct conflict, yet any miscalculation could disrupt this balance.
