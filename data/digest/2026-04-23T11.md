## 中文摘要

> ⚠️ 本摘要覆盖4月13日（监控暂停）至4月23日期间积累的重大事件，非单小时窗口。

### 核心事件

- **特朗普无期限延长停火**：4月22日特朗普在Truth Social宣布将原定到期的停火协议无限期延长，应巴基斯坦请求，给伊朗时间提出"统一方案"。白宫发言人Leavitt确认无新期限，称"牌在特朗普手中"。
- **伊朗扣押霍尔木兹海峡两艘货船**：4月23日伊朗IRGC对三艘货船开火，扣押希腊籍Epaminondas和巴拿马籍MSC-Francesca，称其"未经授权通行且篡改导航系统"。希腊外长确认Epaminondas遭攻击但无法确认被扣押。UAE籍Euphoria遇袭后安全驶抵阿联酋港口。
- **伊斯兰堡和谈搁置**：美国副总统万斯原定率团赴伊斯兰堡，但伊朗未承诺出席，万斯最终留在华盛顿。巴基斯坦总理谢里夫表示将继续斡旋努力。

### 军事动态

- 美国海军部封锁伊朗港口持续，CENTCOM称已拦截28艘船只（截至4月22日）
- 伊朗在霍尔木兹海峡通行量维持在约10%低位
- IRGC发布突击队登船扣押货船的戏剧性画面
- 美国海军部长John Phelan突然离职（无公开理由），由海军次长Hung Cao代理。数周前国防部长Hegseth也曾要求陆军参谋长Randy George辞职

### 外交进展

- 伊朗首席谈判代表Ghalibaf称"不可能重新开放海峡"，指责美国封锁和以色列"全方位好战"严重违反停火
- 伊朗总统Pezeshkian表示伊朗仍愿意谈判，但"违反承诺、封锁和威胁是真谈判的障碍"
- 白宫称伊朗扣押商船不构成违反停火（"不是美国船也不是以色列船"）
- 特朗普称伊朗政权"严重分裂"，暗示华盛顿不确定谁在真正掌控伊朗
- 中国持续外交斡旋：习近平与阿联酋、沙特王储通话，推动和谈；同时推动电动车出口多元化应对中东市场萎缩

### 分析判断

停火进入"封锁战"阶段：双方不再大规模军事对抗，但通过海上封锁和扣押商船博弈控制权。特朗普选择无期限延期是务实之举——既避免了不受欢迎的军事升级，又维持经济压力。但和谈僵局表明短期难以达成突破。伊朗利用海峡控制权作为杠杆，而美国试图用封锁拖垮伊朗经济。这场消耗战的代价正在向全球蔓延：油价上涨冲击中国制造业，中东电动车市场近乎停滞。5月中美峰会可能是打破僵局的关键变量。

## English Summary

> ⚠️ This digest covers major events accumulated between April 13 (monitoring paused) and April 23, not a single-hour window.

### Core Events

- **Trump extends ceasefire indefinitely**: On April 22, Trump announced via Truth Social an open-ended extension of the ceasefire, originally set to expire April 23, at Pakistan's request to give Iran time to formulate a "unified proposal." White House Press Secretary Leavitt confirmed no new deadline, stating "the cards are in President Trump's hands."
- **Iran seizes two cargo ships in Strait of Hormuz**: On April 23, IRGC forces fired on three cargo vessels and seized the Greek-owned Epaminondas and Panama-flagged MSC-Francesca, accusing them of "operating without authorization and tampering with navigation systems." Greece's foreign minister confirmed the attack on Epaminondas but could not confirm seizure. UAE-owned Euphoria was attacked but safely reached port in the UAE.
- **Islamabad peace talks stall**: VP Vance was set to lead the US delegation but Iran never committed to attending. Vance remained in Washington. PM Sharif said Pakistan would "continue earnest efforts" for a negotiated settlement.

### Military Developments

- US naval blockade of Iranian ports continues; CENTCOM reports 28 vessels turned away or returned to port (as of April 22)
- Hormuz Strait traffic remains at ~10% of normal levels
- IRGC released dramatic footage of commandos boarding seized vessels
- Navy Secretary John Phelan ousted with no reason given; Navy Undersecretary Hung Cao serves as acting secretary. This follows Defense Secretary Hegseth's request for Army Chief Randy George to step down weeks earlier

### Diplomatic Developments

- Iran's chief negotiator Ghalibaf said reopening the strait is "not possible" due to US blockade and Israeli "warmongering on all fronts"
- President Pezeshkian said Iran remains open to talks but "breach of commitments, blockade and threats are main obstacles"
- White House said Iran's seizure of commercial ships does not constitute a ceasefire violation ("not US ships, not Israeli ships")
- Trump described Iran's regime as "seriously fractured," suggesting Washington is uncertain who is truly in charge
- China continues diplomatic mediation: Xi held calls with UAE and Saudi crown princes; Chinese EV exporters diversify away from Middle East as exports there nearly halted

### Analysis

The ceasefire has entered a "blockade war" phase: no large-scale military confrontation, but both sides vie for control through maritime blockade and ship seizures. Trump's indefinite extension is pragmatic — avoiding an unpopular military escalation while maintaining economic pressure. However, the stalemate suggests a breakthrough is elusive in the short term. Iran leverages strait control as its primary bargaining chip while the US tries to strangle Iran's economy. The costs of this attrition war are spreading globally: rising oil prices hit Chinese manufacturing, and Middle East EV exports have nearly ground to a halt. The upcoming May US-China summit could be a key variable for breaking the deadlock.
